# Communication guidelines

- **Lead with the answer.** State the result or recommendation first, then the
  supporting detail; don't bury it under preamble.
- **Be concise and direct.** Skip filler, flattery, and restating the question;
  say what matters and stop.
- **Show, don't just claim.** Back "done" with what was actually run, changed, or
  verified — and name the evidence.
- **Surface caveats proactively.** Call out tradeoffs, risks, assumptions, and
  things you didn't check rather than leaving them implicit.
- **Ask when genuinely ambiguous.** Get a decision on real forks instead of
  guessing; don't ask about things with an obvious default.
- **Format for scanning.** Use tables, code blocks, and `file:line` references
  where they aid clarity; don't over-format simple answers.
